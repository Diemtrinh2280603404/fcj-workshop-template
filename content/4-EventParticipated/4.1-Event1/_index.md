# Summary Report: "Saturday Meet up"

&emsp;**Event Name:** Saturday Meet up

&emsp;**Date & Time:** 09:00 – 12:00, May 30, 2026

&emsp;**Location:** 26th Floor, Bitexco Tower, 02 Hai Trieu Street, Saigon Ward, Ho Chi Minh City

&emsp;**Role:** Attendee

---

| # | Speaker | Topic |
|---|---------|-------|

---

### Key Highlights

#### Talk 1 – Huynh Thai Linh: Level Up Your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and Floci

- Guidance for beginners on overcoming concerns about AWS costs and resource management
- Introduction to the free hands-on platform **AWS Cloud Quest** for learning through practice
- Introduction to the simulation tool **Floci** to optimize architecture testing without incurring costs

#### Talk 2 – "The Ballers" Team: Hackathon Journey

- Shared the experience of building two real-world projects under tight time constraints:
  - **SynthHunter** – an AI voice verification system
  - **Vortex** – a platform connecting recruitment journeys
- Key lessons about learning by doing and persevering through challenges under pressure

#### Talk 3 – Nguyễn Thị Quỳnh Như: Why We Always Need Confidence

- Analyzed confidence as an essential factor for career and university life
- Identified hidden barriers: self-doubt and fear of judgment often disguised as quietness
- Provided practical methods to overcome fears and build lasting confidence

#### Talk 4 – Nghĩa Trần: Startup Platform "Tu Vi Dai Viet"

- Introduced an AI platform combining **Tu Vi Dau So** and **Bat Tu** to decode destiny in an accessible and systematic way
- Targeted Gen Z with affordable pricing
- Presented the strategy of migrating infrastructure to **AWS** to improve scalability and cost efficiency

#### Talk 5 – Tran Minh Quan: The Hidden Iceberg of a Project: DevOps Before Disaster

- Warned about the "hidden iceberg" in software projects: seemingly simple features can cause production failures, missed deadlines and team burnout
- Highlighted the consequences of lacking a rigorous DevOps process
- Emphasized the importance of CI/CD, monitoring and early testing

#### Talk 6 – Khoi Pham: The Iceberg of Procrastination

- Demonstrated that procrastination is not laziness but stems from hidden fears (fear of failure, fear of judgment)
- Used psychological data to analyze the root causes of procrastination
- Encouraged taking action through small steps to build momentum and confidence

---

### Key Takeaways

#### Technical Skills
- Learned how to use **AWS Cloud Quest** to practice AWS for free without worrying about unexpected costs
- Understood the real-world product development process in a Hackathon: ideation → prototype → demo
- Grasped the importance of **DevOps** in preventing project risks early on

#### Soft Skills
- Gained greater awareness of the role of **confidence** in personal development and career growth
- Understood the nature of **procrastination** and how to overcome it with small, consistent actions
- Learned the spirit of **perseverance and adaptability** through the story of The Ballers team

#### Entrepreneurial Thinking
- Saw how combining **AI and traditional culture** can create unique products (Tu Vi Dai Viet)
- Understood the importance of choosing the right **infrastructure platform** (AWS) to ensure scalability

---

### Event Experience

Attending **Saturday Meet up** was a memorable experience offering diverse perspectives – from AWS techniques and hackathon spirit to psychology and real-world entrepreneurship.

#### Diverse Perspectives
The highlight of the event was the variety of topics: not just technology but also psychology and real startup stories, helping attendees view personal development from multiple angles.

#### Inspiration from Real Stories
- The story of **The Ballers** team about their hackathon journey was deeply inspiring: despite difficulties and time pressure, the team delivered and gained valuable lessons.
- **Nghia Tran's** presentation showed how AI can be creatively applied to traditional fields, opening a unique path for young entrepreneurs.

#### New Self-Awareness
- The talks on **confidence** and **procrastination** helped me reflect on my own hidden fears and gave me more motivation to take action.

#### Lessons Learned
- **Learning by doing** is always more effective than theory – AWS Cloud Quest and Floci are great tools to get started.
- **DevOps is not optional** but a mandatory requirement to ensure long-term project success.
- **Confidence and small actions** are the keys to overcoming procrastination and fear.
- Young people can absolutely **combine technology and culture** to create truly valuable products.
