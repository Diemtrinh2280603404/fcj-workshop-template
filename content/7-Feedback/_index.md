Here I would like to freely share my personal opinions about my experience participating in the First Cloud AI Journey program, to help the FCAJ team improve any shortcomings based on the following aspects:

---

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**

The working environment at FCAJ is very friendly and open. Team members are always willing to help whenever I encounter difficulties, even outside working hours. The workspace is tidy and comfortable, helping me focus better. However, I think it would be beneficial to add more social gatherings or team bonding activities so that members can get to know each other better.

**2. Support from Mentor / Team Admin**

The mentor provides very detailed guidance, explains clearly whenever I don't understand, and always encourages me to ask questions. The admin team supports administrative tasks, provides necessary documents, and creates favorable conditions for me to work effectively. I especially appreciate that the mentor allows me to try and solve problems on my own instead of just providing the answer — this helped me learn much more through each real-world situation.

**3. Relevance of Work to Academic Major**

The tasks I was assigned align very well with the knowledge I learned at university, while also introducing me to many new areas I had never encountered before, such as cloud infrastructure design on AWS, Infrastructure as Code and building microservices systems. This allowed me to both strengthen my foundational knowledge and gain practical skills of high value.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**

Over the 12 weeks of internship, I learned a great deal of new skills: from core AWS services (EC2, S3, VPC, Lambda, RDS, CloudFront...) to building a complete real-world system — the FlashLearn project. Beyond technical skills, I also improved my teamwork, technical documentation writing and professional communication. The mentor frequently shared real-world experience, helping me better plan my career path.

**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit**

The culture at FCAJ is very positive: everyone respects each other, works seriously while still maintaining a friendly atmosphere. When there are urgent deadlines, the whole team works together and supports one another regardless of position or role. This made me feel like a genuine part of the team, even as an intern.

**6. Internship Policies / Benefits**

The program provides $200 AWS credits for hands-on practice, which created excellent conditions for me to learn and experiment in a real environment. In addition, having the opportunity to attend Saturday Meet up sessions and connect with speakers and industry experts is a major advantage that not every internship program offers.

---

### Additional Questions

**What did I find most satisfying during the internship?**

I am most satisfied with having built a complete real-world project from start to finish — from architecture design, programming, to documentation writing and project handover. This is a highly valuable experience that would be difficult to gain in an academic setting.

**What do I think the program should improve for future interns?**

I think it would be beneficial to add short hands-on workshops on advanced topics such as DevSecOps, AWS cost optimization, or more complex serverless architecture patterns, to give interns more opportunities to explore deeper technical knowledge.

**Would I recommend this internship to friends?**

Yes, I would recommend this program to friends. It is an excellent real-world learning environment, especially for those who want to develop a career in Cloud/AWS. The knowledge and experience gained over 12 weeks at FCAJ provide a solid foundation for a career in technology.

---

### Suggestions & Expectations

- Add periodic in-depth technical sharing sessions (tech talks) throughout the program
- Strengthen networking activities between intern batches to expand connections
- I hope to have the opportunity to continue contributing to the FCAJ community after the internship ends
- Overall, this is a very high-quality internship program and I am very grateful to have participated
